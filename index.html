Vincent Cicirello, Ph.D.

Brief Bio:

Dr. Vincent A. Cicirello is a Professor of Computer Science and Information Systems at Stockton University; and is also associated with Stockton University's Computational Science program as well as its Behavioral Neuroscience program. He received a Ph.D. in Robotics from Carnegie Mellon University in 2003, a M.S./B.S. in Computer Science from Drexel University in 1999 (dual M.S./B.S. program), and a B.S. in Mathematics from Drexel University also in 1999. His research interests include artificial intelligence, evolutionary computation, swarm intelligence, and computer science education. He was designated an ACM Senior Member in 2011, received the 2005 AAAI Outstanding Paper award, and was nominated for the 2006 GECCO Best Genetic Algorithm Track award. He received a U.S. Patent in 2010, and has published over 50 journal and conference publications. He is a member of AAAI (Life member), ACM (Senior Member), ACM SIGCSE, ACM SIGAI, ACM SIGEVO, CCSC, IEEE, IEEE Computer Society, IEEE Computational Intelligence Society, and SIAM.
